[ Race weekend ]

Three days. One cannon.

Everything below happens within one tram ride of the start. Bib pickup closes Saturday at 18:00 sharp — there is no race-morning pickup, and the volunteers who enforce that rule have heard every excuse since 1979.

Friday23 Oct

  • 10:00
    Expo opens — Pier 9 Hall. Bib pickup, 60 exhibitors, and a wall printed with all 28,000 names. Find yours.
  • 12:00
    Course talks, hourly. Twenty minutes, no slides: where the hills are, where the wind is, where the crowds carry you.
  • 20:00
    Expo closes. Go to bed. You won't, but go to bed.

Saturday24 Oct

  • 08:00
    Shakeout 5K — Docklands. Easy pace, no timing, coffee at the turnaround. The elites usually show.
  • 09:00
    Expo, day two. Last gels, last pins, last chance to buy the shoes you should not race in tomorrow.
  • 18:00
    Bib pickup closes. Hard stop. Then long tables in Civic Square — the carb night is free with a bib.

Sunday25 Oct — race day

  • 04:30
    Transit runs free for bib holders. Every tram, every bus, straight to the Docklands.
  • 05:30
    Corrals open. A through E, seeded by your entry estimate. Bag drop closes 06:30.
  • 05:45
    Roads close, city-wide. They reopen rolling, behind the 7:30 / km sweep bus.
  • 06:50
    Wheelchair start. The fastest anyone moves through Arden all year.
  • 07:00
    The cannon. One shot from the harbour battery. You'll feel it in your chest. Run.
  • 14:30
    Course closes — 7 h 30 cutoff. The finish festival in Civic Square keeps going until the last band stops.
